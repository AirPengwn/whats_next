Career focus areas
Erin is positioned for entry-level roles in wetland science, water quality, GIS, wildlife biology, and environmental consulting. The notes below outline the strongest matches and where to look.
Strongest entry-level fits

Wetland Scientist / Delineator — Environmental consulting firms (ECS, Kimley-Horn, Terracon, Kleinfelder, WSP) hire environmental scientists with wetland delineation training. Direct match for GEOL 455 and Army Corps methods. Often titled "Environmental Scientist I" or "Staff Environmental Scientist."

GIS Technician / Analyst — Local governments, conservation organizations, and consulting firms hire GIS technicians. NRM 344 + GEOG 221 + suitability analysis experience is a solid foundation. Look for "GIS Analyst I" or "Geospatial Analyst" titles.

Water Quality Technician / Field Scientist — State agencies, EPA, USGS, and consulting firms. Direct match for ENVH 310 and field sampling skills. Titles: "Environmental Technician," "Water Quality Specialist," "Environmental Analyst."

Wildlife Biologist / Field Technician — State wildlife agencies, USFWS, conservation organizations. Match for NRM 479 and NRM 330 field skills. Titles: "Wildlife Biologist I," "Biological Science Technician," "Field Technician."

Key employers to target

Federal: EPA (Region 4), USGS, USFWS, Army Corps of Engineers, USDA Forest Service, NRCS

State: NC DEQ, NC Wildlife Resources Commission, SC DHEC, TDEC (Tennessee), GADNR (Georgia)

Consulting: ECS Federal, Terracon, WSP, Kimley-Horn, Cardno, Brown and Caldwell, Stantec

Nonprofits: The Nature Conservancy, American Rivers, Southern Appalachian Highlands Conservancy, Ducks Unlimited, NRDC
