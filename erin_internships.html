Why this is a top match

SAWSC is the USGS Water Science Center for NC, SC, and GA — exactly where Erin studied and conducted her Hurricane Helene research. SAWSC technicians collect streamflow data, water quality samples, and biological specimens from rivers, streams, and aquifers across the region. What hydrologic technicians do

Operate and maintain stream gauges; collect water quality samples; conduct aquatic biological surveys; analyze field and lab data; support hydrologists on research projects. Physical fieldwork in diverse weather conditions required.

About the program

The USGS Recent Graduate Program is a formal federal hiring pathway. Participants complete a structured 1–2 year developmental experience, receive mentoring, and can be non-competitively converted to permanent federal positions at the end. This is a meaningful career accelerator for someone targeting a federal water resources or environmental science career.

How to find the right position

Search USAJobs for "USGS Recent Graduate" and filter by series: 1315 (Hydrology), 1350 (Geology), 0401 (Biology), 0408 (Ecology). Also search 1301 (Physical Science). Prioritize Water Science Center positions — SAWSC (NC/SC/GA), Southeast (FL/AL/MS), and Appalachians WSC are the strongest regional fits.
