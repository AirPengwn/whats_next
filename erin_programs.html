About funded MS programs
Research & Teaching Assistantships — how they work

A Research Assistantship (RA) means working on a professor's funded research project. In exchange: full tuition waiver + annual stipend ($18k–$35k depending on school) + health insurance. A Teaching Assistantship (TA) means helping teach undergrad courses — same package. Both are most common at the PhD level but many MS programs offer them, especially thesis-track MS degrees at R1 universities.

Most important action: email professors directly before applying. Many funded MS positions are filled through personal contact and never publicly posted. A short, targeted email referencing a professor's specific research and explaining how Erin's background connects is the single highest-leverage action she can take.

Two pathways at UGA

Odum School of Ecology MS: One of the premier ecology graduate programs in the US. Competitive; requires strong GPA and research experience. Erin's 3.731 GPA and capstone research are strong credentials. Browse faculty at odum.uga.edu.

Savannah River Ecology Lab (SREL): Field station near Aiken, SC. Research in aquatic-terrestrial linkages, contaminant ecology, herpetology, and radioecology. Graduate students enroll in UGA Odum School while based at SREL. Strong match for Erin's contaminated rivers and water quality background.

Browse Odum School faculty at odum.uga.edu/people and SREL researchers at srel.uga.edu/people. Contact 2–3 faculty directly with CV and Pigeon River research summary before applying.
