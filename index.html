Why this profile is competitive

Erin graduates with a rare combination of rigorous interdisciplinary coursework and genuine hands-on field experience across wetlands, water quality, wildlife ecology, and GIS — disciplines that rarely overlap in a single undergraduate. Her WCU Environmental Science program is intentionally interdisciplinary, and she took full advantage of it, building a coherent research track from General Chemistry through Environmental Toxicology, Contaminated Rivers, Wetlands, Mammalogy, and two GIS courses.

Her capstone research project — analyzing lead contamination in Pigeon River Basin sediments following Hurricane Helene (2024) — applied graduate-level geomorphological-geochemical methodology to a real, ongoing environmental disaster in her own backyard. That project will anchor every application she submits.

Her GIS proficiency (introductory + applied, including a multi-criteria suitability analysis capstone) is increasingly essential in ecological research, and her environmental justice coursework in the Appalachian context gives her a policy and community dimension that purely technical candidates lack.

She also volunteered in Hurricane Helene disaster relief in the same region she was studying — a rare connection between scholarship and service. Her 3.731 GPA with Dean's and Chancellor's List honors places her above the typical 3.0–3.2 minimum for funded research assistantships at R1 universities.
