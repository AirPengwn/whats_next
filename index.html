Why this profile is competitive

Erin graduates with a rare combination of rigorous interdisciplinary coursework and genuine hands-on field experience across wetlands, water quality, wildlife ecology, and GIS — disciplines that rarely overlap in a single undergraduate. Her WCU Environmental Science program is intentionally interdisciplinary, and she took full advantage of it, building a coherent research track that runs from General Chemistry through Environmental Toxicology, Contaminated Rivers, Wetlands, Mammalogy, and two GIS courses.

Her capstone research project — analyzing lead contamination in Pigeon River Basin sediments following Hurricane Helene (2024) — is not a typical undergraduate exercise. It applied graduate-level geomorphological-geochemical methodology (from a course cross-listed at the 500 level) to a real, ongoing environmental disaster in her own backyard. That project will anchor every application she submits.

Her GIS proficiency (both introductory and applied courses, including a multi-criteria suitability analysis capstone) is increasingly essential in ecological research, and her environmental justice coursework in the Appalachian context gives her a policy and community dimension that purely technical candidates lack.

She also volunteered in Hurricane Helene disaster relief in the same region she was simultaneously studying — a connection between scholarship and service that is rare and worth highlighting prominently in any personal statement or cover letter. Her 3.731 GPA with Dean's and Chancellor's List honors places her above the typical 3.0–3.2 minimum for funded research assistantships at R1 universities.
